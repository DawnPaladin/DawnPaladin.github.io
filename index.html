James Harris

Designer and Front-End Developer

Hi there! I'm James Harris, a designer and front-end developer specializing in tackling complex concepts and making them easy to understand.

I love studying how games and stories work and figuring out how they influence people. I'm passionate about using technology to improve the world on a global scale.

I'm trained and experienced in graphic design and the use of Adobe Creative Suite. I'm presently teaching myself how to be a full-stack developer.

Stories make use of recurring ideas called "tropes" which repeat across genres and through time. The community at TVTropes.org catalogues these tropes in an enormous wiki, collecting examples for every imaginable trope with light-hearted glee. I created the Periodic Table of Storytelling as an introduction to TVTropes; it's an infographic that organizes the basic building blocks of storytelling into a familiar format.

Featured on Gizmodo, io9, The A.V. Club, Boing Boing, Laughing Squid, Fast Company, and Design Taxi, with over 1.3 million views.

My Father's World has a very unusual curriculum system which saves parents a lot of time but can be difficult to explain. So I designed and coded an interactive infographic that uses HTML5 <canvas> to first show parents how it works and then let them play around it as much as they like.

Part coding exercise, part writing project, I made this to help me codify some ethical principles I've been developing in my own life.
